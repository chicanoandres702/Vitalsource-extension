Week 9 Overview: Understanding Child Abuse and Neglect

As you work through this week's activities, think about the following questions:

  • How can you work, as a social worker, to prevent child abuse and neglect?
  • What does the social worker's role as a mandated reporter mean with regard to individual and family clients when abuse and neglect are suspected?

Child abuse and neglect are serious public health problems that can have long-term impacts on health, opportunity, and wellbeing. As mandated reporters, social workers must understand how to assess the signs and symptoms of child abuse and apply strategies of prevention. Types of child abuse includes any act or failure by a parent or a caregiver that results in actual or potential harm to a child that can occur in a child's home, at school, or in communities the child interacts with. This week, you will learn how to recognize the signs and symptoms of child abuse as well as methods of prevention.
